I'm Erin Stephens — researcher, forensic analyst, and doctoral candidate in forensic cyberpsychology. My work lives at the crossroads of how people behave, how they hide, and how they get found.

What I do

Forensic Research

Behavioral and financial pattern analysis of offenders operating in cryptocurrency and dark web environments. Open-source intelligence (OSINT), cryptocurrency forensics, offender behavior profiling.

OSINT & Crypto Forensics

Tracing financial patterns and digital footprints across open and closed-source data. Following the money where it tries not to be followed.

Law Enforcement Training

Training facilitation for cybercrime investigations and Internet Crimes Against Children (ICAC). Curriculum design and delivery for government grant-funded programs at Boston University.

Research & Analysis

Research design, technical writing, and report preparation for academic, government, and legal contexts. Grant compliance, peer-reviewed publication support, expert analysis.

The thread connecting everything I've done: follow the pattern long enough, and the truth surfaces.
