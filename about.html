A researcher who follows the pattern.

My career has always been about the same thing: patterns. Who does what, why, and how they try to cover their tracks. I started with anthropology at Berkeley — studying human trade networks and behavioral systems. Then financial software, where I turned cash flow patterns into tools people could actually use. Now I'm in forensic cyberpsychology, applying the same pattern-recognition lens to the darkest corners of the internet.
